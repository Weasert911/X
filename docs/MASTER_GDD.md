## 2. Executive Overview

### 2.1 Core Idea
"YOU MUST EAT" is a focused, atmospheric narrative FPS centered on Arjun Kale, a disgraced Ex-Crime Branch detective. After his 9-year-old niece, Priya, is kidnapped by a sophisticated trafficking ring known as "The Caterers" in the slums of Dharavi, Mumbai, Arjun returns to the only place he knows how to navigate. He must move through the shadows, fight the Syndicate, and dismantle the systemic corruption that destroyed his life and took his family.

### 2.2 Target Experience
The game is designed as a tight, high-tension experience where the player is often the underdog. It is NOT a power fantasy or an open-world RPG. Instead, it is a focused descent into a living nightmare, emphasizing:
- **Atmosphere:** Oppressive, claustrophobic, and visually striking.
- **Stealth:** Tactical movement and the use of shadows as a primary tool for survival.
- **Narrative:** A personal story of grief, redemption, and the fight against systemic injustice.
- **Combat:** Brutal, impactful, and risky. Every shot fired is a potential death sentence.

### 2.3 Player Fantasy
- **The Disgraced Professional:** The player feels the weight of Arjun's past. He has the skills of a top detective but the resources of a ghost.
- **The Shadow Hunter:** Mastering the "Hashing Shadows" mechanic provides a feeling of predatory power within a vulnerable state.
- **The Desperate Protector:** The drive to rescue Priya fuels every action, creating a sense of urgency and emotional stakes.
- **The Truth-Seeker:** Uncovering the conspiracy and gathering evidence to break the silence of a city.

### 2.4 Core Pillars
1. **The Horror of Silence:** The world is defined not by what is said, but by what is ignored. Systemic complicity is the true antagonist.
2. **High-Contrast Existence:** Visual and narrative duality—the filth of the slum vs. the sterile luxury of the elite; the safety of the shadow vs. the danger of the light.
3. **Weighted Consequences:** Every action has a cost. Stamina, ammo, and health are scarce, making every decision tactical.

## 3. World Setting & Lore

### 3.1 Dharavi: The Living Organism
Dharavi is more than a setting; it is a character. 1 square kilometre, 1.2 million people. To the world, it is a place of poverty. To the residents, it is a complex ecosystem of survival.
- **The Veins:** The narrow, winding alleyways that form the circulatory system of the slum.
- **The Rooftops:** A secondary layer of movement consisting of corrugated tin roofs, providing tactical vantage points.
- **The Silence:** A cultural agreement of survival where people look away from the "Caterers" to protect their own.

### 3.2 The Syndicate: "The Caterers"
They are a predatory entity that operates on the principle of hunger—both literal and metaphorical. They don't use force first; they use hopelessness.

#### Organizational Hierarchy:
1. **The Scouts:** The eyes and ears. Local kids, tea stall owners, and the homeless. They are the most dangerous because they are invisible.
2. **The Fixers:** The logistics. They manage the "nests," transport the children, and handle the violence.
3. **The Upper Tier:** The architects. Faceless figures and political puppets who use trafficking as a tool for leverage and power.

## 4. Story & Narrative Arc

### 4.1 Protagonist: Arjun Kale
- **Background:** 34-year-old Ex-Crime Branch detective. Once the gold standard of Mumbai Police, he was framed and dismissed after uncovering evidence linking a Cabinet Minister to the Caterers.
- **State of Mind:** "Broken Good." He is a man who has lost everything—his career, his reputation, and his sobriety. His fight for Priya is his last attempt at redemption.
- **Motivation:** Pure, desperate love for his niece and a dormant, burning rage against the system that betrayed him.

### 4.2 Narrative Structure (The 5 Acts)

#### Act I: The Return (The Veins)
Act I begins during the day. Arjun is lying drunk in the street outside his rented house. A man nearby kicks him and tells him to go elsewhere. Arjun returns to his house and falls asleep, eventually waking up at night to the sound of his phone ringing. From here, he returns to Dharavi, navigating the narrowest veins and avoiding patrols.
- **Goal:** Locate an old informant.
- **Climax:** Finding the informant dead. The realization that the Syndicate knows he is back.

#### Act II: The Scout (The Market)
As the slum wakes, Arjun tracks Munia, a low-level fixer, through the chaotic morning markets.
- **Goal:** Find the "Ledger" of the Caterers.
- **Climax:** Finding Priya's name in the ledger. Confirmation that she has been "transferred" to a more secure location.

#### Act III: The Safe House (The Textile Unit)
Arjun breaches a holding facility—an abandoned textile factory.
- **Goal:** Rescue the children in the "nest."
- **Climax:** A phone call from a mid-level fixer revealing that Priya has been moved to "The Feast," the central hub of the Syndicate.

#### Act IV: The Descent (The Contrast)
Arjun moves from the grime of the slum to the sterile luxury of Mumbai's political elite.
- **Goal:** Infiltrate the circles of power to locate "The Feast."
- **Climax:** Identifying the Cabinet Minister as the head of the operation and recovering the evidence lost three years ago.

#### Act V: The Feast (The Final Nest)
The finale takes place in a hidden complex beneath a luxury hotel, bordering the slum.
- **Goal:** Infiltrate the hub and rescue Priya.
- **Climax:** A final confrontation where Arjun chooses Priya over his own professional redemption, ensuring the evidence is leaked to the world, permanently breaking the silence of Dharavi.

## 5. High-Level Game Mechanics

### 5.1 Core Gameplay Loop
`Infiltration` $\rightarrow$ `Investigation/Stealth` $\rightarrow$ `Tactical Combat (Optional/Risky)` $\rightarrow$ `Narrative Revelation` $\rightarrow$ `Progression`.

### 5.2 Key Systems
- **Shadow Merge (Hashing Shadows):** The central tactical pillar. Arjun blends into high-contrast shadows to vanish from enemy sight.
- **Weighted Combat:** Every bullet counts. Gunplay is precise, lethal, and terrifying.
- **Environmental Investigation:** Interacting with the world to uncover the location of nests and the identity of the Upper Tier.

## 6. Art & Audio Direction (Summary)
- **Visual Style:** High-Contrast Black, White, and Red (Hashing Shadows). A raw, sketch-like aesthetic.
- **Camera:** Visceral First-Person perspective with subtle head bob and high-impact feedback.
- **Audio:** A mix of "City Hum" (Mumbai background noise) and oppressive silence, accented by distorted Indian classical instruments.
