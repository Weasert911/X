## 2. Movement & Traversal

### 2.1 Core Movement States
| State | Speed | Noise | Purpose |
|-------|-------|-------|---------|
| **Sprint** | 1.5x Walk | High | Rapid repositioning / Escape |
| **Walk** | 1.0x | Low | Tactical exploration |
| **Crouch** | 0.6x Walk | Minimal | Stealth infiltration / Accuracy |

#### Mechanics:
- **Stamina System:** Sprinting consumes stamina rapidly. Exhaustion leads to a brief recovery period where Arjun cannot sprint or jump.
- **Verticality:** Use of "rooftop" navigation. Ledge climbing and waist-high obstacle leaping are essential for bypassing guards.
- **Hide & Cover:** Contextual cover system. Pressing the hide button near walls or debris reduces Arjun's profile and allows "peeking" to observe enemy patterns without full exposure.

## 3. Stealth & Shadow Mechanics

### 3.1 Shadow Merge (Hashing Shadows)
The core tactical mechanic of the game. Arjun utilizes the extreme contrast of the environment to vanish.

**Implementation:**
- **Trigger:** When standing in "Deep Black" areas (determined by the Hashing Shadows shader/lighting), Arjun can enter a "Merge" state.
- **Effect:** While merged, he is nearly invisible to enemies unless they are in immediate proximity.
- **Shadow Meter:** Merging consumes a resource. This meter regenerates when in light or when stationary, forcing the player to move between shadows.
- **Risk:** Moving while merged reduces the effectiveness of the cloak and increases the noise generated.

### 3.2 Detection System
A high-tension system based on a combination of visual and audio cues.

**Detection States:**
1. **Unaware:** Enemy follows a set patrol route.
2. **Suspicious:** Enemy hears a sound or sees a flicker. They move to investigate the last known position.
3. **Alerted:** Enemy has spotted the player. They call for backup and move to engage.
4. **Combat:** Full engagement. Enemies coordinate flanking and suppression fire.

**Detection Factors:**
- **Visual:** Line-of-sight based. Red highlights indicate active searching.
- **Audio:** Noise "ripples" generated by sprinting, shooting, or interacting with physics objects (e.g., knocking over a tin can).

## 4. Combat & Weapon Systems

### 4.1 Combat Philosophy
Combat is designed to be a last resort. Arjun is a professional, but he is outnumbered and outgunned. Gunplay is weighted, purposeful, and lethal.

### 4.2 Weapon Architecture (Modular System)
To ensure scalability and clean save/load integration, the weapon system is split into three layers:

#### Layer 1: WeaponData (Resource - CONFIG)
Pure data containers (`.tres`) defining the weapon's identity.
- **Stored Data:** `gun_id`, `weapon_type` (Pistol, SMG, Rifle), `mag_size`, `max_mags`, `reload_time`, `fire_rate`, `recoil`, `spread`, `damage`.

#### Layer 2: Weapon (Scene + Script - BEHAVIOR)
Handles the actual gameplay logic and visuals.
- **Responsibilities:** Raycast shooting, animation playback (shoot, reload, draw), recoil/spread application, and runtime ammo state (`ammo_in_mag`, `mags_left`).

#### Layer 3: WeaponManager (Control + State)
The central controller for the player's arsenal.
- **Responsibilities:** Weapon switching, equip/unequip logic, reload requests, and communication with the `GameManager` via signals.

### 4.3 Weapon Categories & Gear
- **Improvised Melee:** Rusted pipes, knives, scrap metal. Used for silent takedowns and desperation fighting.
- **Tactical Handgun:** Precise, reliable, and relatively quiet. The primary tool for tactical strikes.
- **High-Impact (Rifle/Shotgun):** Used for breaching nests and dealing with groups of Fixers. High noise, high damage.
- **Tactical Gear:** Distraction items used to manipulate enemy patrol paths.

### 4.4 Combat Mechanics
- **Recoil & Spread:** Significant weapon kick requires control. ADS (Aim Down Sights) is mandatory for precision.
- **Critical Hits:** Headshots deal massive damage, reflecting Arjun's tactical training.
- **Ammo Scarcity:** Ammo is limited, forcing a reliance on stealth and melee.
- **Manual Reloading:** Tactical pauses that create high-tension moments during firefights.

## 5. Interaction & Physics Systems

### 5.1 Object Interaction
The `Interact` system allows Arjun to engage with the world:
- **Dialogue:** Branching conversations with residents. Choices reflect Arjun's "Broken Good" alignment.
- **Investigation:** Searching for "The Ledger" and other clues to unlock new locations.
- **Scavenging:** Picking up limited ammo and improvised weapons.

### 5.2 Pick & Throw System
A minimal physics-based system for interacting with RigidBody3D objects.

**Mechanics:**
- **Pick Up:** Look at a `pickable` object (crosshair turns green) and press `Interact (E)`.
- **Hold:** Only one object can be held at a time. The object is held in front of the camera via physics constraints.
- **Drop:** Press `Interact (E)` while holding an object.
- **Throw:** Press `Throw (Q)` to apply a physics impulse to the object in the direction the player is facing.

**Use Cases:**
- Throwing objects to create noise and distract guards.
- Clearing obstacles from a path.
- Tactical use of improvised projectiles.

## 6. Enemy AI & Hierarchy

### 6.1 Enemy Types
| Type | Role | Behavior | Stats |
|------|------|----------|--------|
| **Scouts** | Watchers | Basic patrols. Alert others immediately upon spotting the player. | Low HP, High Numbers |
| **Fixers** | Enforcers | Use cover and flanking tactics. Aggressively hunt alerted players. | Mid HP, Ranged Weapons |
| **Upper Tier**| Bosses | Coordinate attacks, set traps, and use psychological warfare via dialogue. | High HP, Specialized Gear |

### 6.2 AI Behavior Tree
- **Patrol:** Routine movement patterns that the player can learn and exploit.
- **Investigate:** Systematic searching of the area surrounding the last known player position.
- **Combat:** Coordinated flanking, suppression fire, and tactical retreats to better cover.

## 7. Progression & Mastery

### 7.1 Narrative Progression
The game follows a strict Act-based structure (Acts 1-5). Progression is gated by narrative milestones and the discovery of key evidence (e.g., The Ledger).

### 7.2 Player Mastery (Non-RPG)
There are no XP, skill trees, or stat upgrades. Progression is purely based on player skill:
- **Stealth Mastery:** Learning to navigate the "Deep Black" and timing the Shadow Merge.
- **Combat Proficiency:** Mastering recoil control and enemy flanking patterns.
- **Environmental Knowledge:** Understanding the layout of Dharavi to find shortcuts and hiding spots.

## 8. Save System Integration
Weapon and interaction states are persisted via the `WeaponManager` and `GameManager`.

**Stored Data Structure:**
```json
"weapons": {
  "current_weapon_id": "smg_01",
  "inventory": {
    "smg_01": { "ammo_in_mag": 18, "mags_left": 2 },
    "pistol_01": { "ammo_in_mag": 6, "mags_left": 1 }
  }
}
```
*Note: Static weapon stats (damage, fire rate) are NOT saved, as they are retrieved from the `WeaponData` resource on load.*
