# YOU MUST EAT
## Full Story & Game Lore Bible
### FPS | Dharavi, Mumbai | 13+

---

## WORLD SETTING

Dharavi. 1 square kilometre. 1.2 million people.

The world outside sees tin roofs, open drains, and poverty. The people inside see home. They see chai stalls opening at 4am. They see grandmothers hanging clothes across alleyways. They see children playing cricket with a plastic ball between parked auto rickshaws. This is not a place of victims. This is a place that survives.

But beneath the noise, beneath the colour, beneath the million small human lives that make this city breathe—something runs.

Children vanish. Not every day. Not in numbers that make the news. One here. Two there. A girl whose mother works 16-hour shifts. A boy whose father drank himself to death. No one reports them at first. When they do, police write notes in books that get filed away. NGOs make reports that no one reads. Local leaders say they went to good schools. Factory jobs. Relatives in Delhi.

Most never come back.

No one talks about it out loud. Everyone knows.

"In Dharavi, every shadow hides something. Every alley has ears. You learn to move with the darkness. Or you become part of it."

## PROTAGONIST: ARJUN KALE

34. Ex-Crime Branch, Mumbai Police.

He was good. Too good. Decorated. Fast-tracked. The kind of cop that actually did the work. Three years ago, he broke a trafficking ring. He followed the money all the way up and found evidence that led directly to a cabinet minister.

He submitted the file.

Within 72 hours:
- The evidence locker caught fire.
- His partner was transferred to another state.
- He was suspended on fabricated conduct charges.
- Six months later, he was dismissed.

He drank for a year. Lived in a rented room on the other side of the city. Stopped answering calls. Stopped going outside.

Then his sister called.

Priya was missing. His 9-year-old niece. Taken from outside their home in Dharavi. The same slum Arjun grew up in. The same network. The same silence.

That night, he packed a bag.

No badge. No backup. No warrant. No rules. Just a man who knows exactly how these people operate. And a debt he intends to collect. He does not believe he will survive this. He does not care.

**Alignment:** Lawful Good $\rightarrow$ Broken Good
**Core Rule:** He will not hurt civilians. He will not hesitate with anyone else.

---

## THE ANTAGONISTS: THE CATERERS

They don't have a logo. They don't have guns on every corner. They don't yell threats. That's how you know they are real.

Locals only whisper about them as 'The Caterers'.

Because they always know. They know who is hungry enough to take an offer. They know who won't ask questions. They know which parent will accept 5,000 rupees and look the other way. They know which child will not fight back.

They do not use force first. They use hunger. They use hopelessness. They use promises. The violence only comes when someone tries to leave.

The operation is perfectly layered:
1. **Scouts** - local kids, homeless people, tea stall owners. They watch. They report. No one suspects them.
2. **Fixers** - the ones that make the arrangements. Drive the vans. Lock the doors.
3. **The Upper Tier** - faceless. Known only by nicknames. Burner phones. This is the layer that got Arjun fired. This is the layer no one ever sees.

"You think you are fighting men. You are fighting hunger. You are fighting silence. You cannot kill either."

## FULL STORY ARC

### ACT I: THE RETURN
Arjun returns to Dharavi at 2:17am under a shroud of rain. He navigates the narrowest veins of the slum, rediscovering the rhythm of the streets. He avoids patrols and scavenges basic tools. 

The act culminates when he reaches his old informant's door. He finds the man dead—a professional execution. The message is clear: the Syndicate knows he is back. Arjun realizes that his return hasn't gone unnoticed; he has stepped back into a game where the rules have changed.

### ACT II: THE SCOUT
As the slum wakes, Arjun tracks Munia, a low-level fixer. He follows Munia through the chaotic morning markets, avoiding detection amidst the civilian crowds. The pursuit leads to an abandoned godown where Arjun engages in his first major firefight.

Upon Munia's defeat, Arjun finds a ledger. Among the list of processed children is Priya. The confirmation transforms his mission from a search into a rescue operation. He discovers that Priya was "transferred," meaning she has been moved up the chain to a more secure location.

### ACT III: THE SAFE HOUSE
Arjun breaches a holding facility—an abandoned textile factory. Through tactical combat and stealth, he clears the building and rescues several children. However, Priya is not among them.

As he prepares to leave, a rotary phone rings in an empty office. A voice—the mid-level fixer—mocks him, revealing that he has found the "wrong nest." The voice reveals that Priya has been taken to the "Feast," a high-security location where the Upper Tier operates.

### ACT IV: THE DESCENT
Arjun delves deeper into the political underbelly of Mumbai. He tracks the money from the "Caterers" back to the same cabinet minister who destroyed his career three years ago. He infiltrates high-society parties and government offices, contrasting the luxury of the elite with the grime of Dharavi.

He discovers that the trafficking ring isn't just for profit—it's a tool for leverage, a way for the politician to keep other powerful people compliant. Arjun is no longer just saving his niece; he is gathering the evidence he lost three years ago.

### ACT V: THE FEAST
The finale takes place in a hidden complex beneath a luxury hotel, bordering the slum. The "Feast" is the central hub where the most "valuable" targets are held before being shipped overseas.

Arjun fights his way through the elite guard of the Upper Tier. In a final confrontation with the Cabinet Minister and the head of the Syndicate, Arjun must choose between the evidence that could clear his name and the immediate safety of Priya. 

He chooses Priya. As the facility is compromised, he rescues his niece and ensures the evidence is leaked to every news outlet in the city simultaneously. The "Caterers" are exposed, but Arjun vanishes back into the shadows of Dharavi, no longer a cop, but the man who broke the silence.

## TONE & RATING GUIDELINES (13+)

This is not a game about shooting. This is a game about silence.

1. **Violence:** Present but never gratuitous. Combat is heavy and weighted. Every bullet matters. No dismemberment. No gore. When someone dies, they simply fall.
2. **Trafficking:** The core theme, handled with absolute gravity. Children are never shown in distress or used for shock value. Their situation is conveyed through environmental storytelling: empty rooms, discarded toys, half-eaten meals.
3. **Horror:** The horror is systemic. It is the horror of complicity—the realization that everyone knows, and no one speaks.
4. **Content:** No swearing. No explicit content. The tension is built through atmosphere and the weight of the situation.

The art style is high-contrast black, white, and red. Hashing shadows. A raw sketch aesthetic that abstracts the violence while emphasizing the brutality of the environment.
